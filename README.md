# Research Paper
The exponential growth of ”Cloud Data-Centers” has made operational carbon efficiency a critical priority, necessitating the alignment of workloads execution with low carbon energy availability. While temporal shifting–delaying delay–tolerant batch jobs to greener time windows–effectively reduces carbon intensity, it introduces significant trade-offs in job completion and resource utilization. However, existing state-of-the-art mechanisms often assume deterministic job durations or overlook the financial cost of idling reserved instances during high-carbon periods. This paper proposes a Cost-Aware Probabilistic Temporal Shifting Framework that dynamically schedules workloads by balancing real-time grid carbon intensity against performance and cost constraints. Experimental results using real-world traces demonstrate that the proposed method achieves a significant reduction in carbon emissions while maintaining high throughput compared to static shifting policies.
